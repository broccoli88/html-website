WM Game

In Warmachine, a typical match takes place on a tabletop battlefield with terrain features such as forests, buildings, and obstacles. The game board is typically divided into deployment zones for each player.



In Warmachine, the types of play are categorized based on the number of points allocated to each player's army. The point size determines the size and scale of the battle. There are 4 primary point-levels:

  • Small (0 - 25 pts)
  • Standard (25 - 50 pts)
  • Large (50 - 75 pts)
  • High Command (75+ pts)

Short match overview:

  1. Army Building: Before the match, each player constructs their army using a specified point limit (e.g., 25, 50, or 75 points). They select their warcaster or warlock, warjacks or warbeasts, units, solos, and attachments to form their battle group.

  2. Deployment: Players take turns deploying their models in their respective deployment zones. The location and positioning of units are crucial as they set the stage for the upcoming battle.

  3. Turn Sequence: The game is played over a series of rounds, with each round consisting of two player turns. Players alternate activating their models during their turns.

  4. Activation: During a player's turn, they activate their models one at a time, performing actions such as moving, attacking, casting spells, or using special abilities. Each model has a specific activation cost, meaning players must carefully plan their actions to optimize their strategies.

  5. Combat and Resolution: When models engage in combat, they use their respective weapons and abilities to attack opponents. Attacks are resolved using dice rolls and comparing various statistics. Damage dealt to models may result in the loss of hit points or specific effects, depending on the type of attack.

  6. Scenario Objectives: Many matches include scenario objectives or victory conditions beyond merely eliminating the opponent's forces. Players may need to control specific points on the battlefield, protect key targets, or complete specific objectives to win.

  7. Focus and Fury Management: Warcasters manage focus, while warlocks manage fury during their turns. These resources fuel their abilities, spells, and control over warjacks and warbeasts. Effective resource management is vital for success.

  8. Tactics and Strategy: Players must adapt to the unfolding battle, adjusting their tactics based on their opponent's moves and the changing battlefield conditions. Tactical positioning, threat assessment, and resource allocation play crucial roles in determining the oustcome of the match.

  9. Conclusion: The match concludes when one player achieves the victory conditions, which could be scenario-based or through the complete elimination of the opponent's warcaster or warlock.

Warmachine matches are intense and dynamic, offering a mix of strategic planning, tactical positioning, and thrilling combat, all set in the immersive and rich fantasy world of the Iron Kingdoms.
