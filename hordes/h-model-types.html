Model Types

In Hordes, similar to Warmachine, there are various types of models that make up the forces of each faction. Each model type serves a specific role on the battlefield, and players must strategically use them to achieve victory. As of my last update in September 2021, here are the main model types in Hordes:

  1. Warlock: The central figure in each Hordes army, the warlock is a powerful leader gifted with the ability to control and channel the primal power known as fury. They lead their forces into battle and support their warbeasts with unique spells and abilities.

  2. Warbeasts: The core of a Hordes army, warbeasts are primal creatures led by warlocks. They channel and generate fury, a volatile resource that allows them to unleash powerful attacks and abilities.

  3. Units: Units in Hordes consist of groups of infantry, beasts, or warriors that fight together as a cohesive force. They can be specialized in different roles, such as melee combat, ranged attacks, or support abilities.

  4. Solos: Solos are individual models with unique abilities and often serve as specialists or champions. They can be warlocks' assistants, skilled warriors, or versatile utility models.

  5. Battle Engines: Large and powerful constructs, battle engines are war machines with unique abilities that can drastically impact the battlefield.

  6. Gargantuans: These are massive beasts that tower over regular ones. Gargantuans are extremely powerful and serve as formidable assets in an army
